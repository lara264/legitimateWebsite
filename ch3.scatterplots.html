Scatterplots

    The most effective way to display the relationship between two quantitative variables is a scatterplot.
    A scatterplot plots the data in coordinates without connecting the points.
    To interpret a scatterplot look at:
      Overall Pattern
      • Form: is it linear?
      • Direction: is the slope positive or negative?
      • Strength: how strong is the linear relationship?
      Deviations in the Pattern
      • Outliers: points in the direction of the y axis
      • Influential Points: points in the direction of the x axis
